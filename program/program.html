17th Intenational Theraphosidae Workshop

Rio di Janeiro, Brazil, March 21-25, 2016


2016 Workshop Program

This year we have a range of Program from several different disciplines and there shall be a number of presentations over the course of the 5 days.

Session Timetable

Dr D Johnson


Zoologist, University of Toronto
"Survival traits of the Theraphosidae"

Join renowned Zoologist, Dr D Johnson, as he presents the findings from his research into the survival traits of different species Theraphosidae (Tarantula) family from different parts of the world. Johnson has traveled the world to study various Tarantula species found in Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
Date: 21 March 2016, Doors open at 10am for a 10:30am start.

Return to the top

Professer Trish Sullivan


lecturer at University of Sao Paulo
"Ecosystems in danger"

Professer Trish Sullivan, a prominent lecturer in the Ecology and Environmental Studies department at the University of Sao Paulo, has been carefully studying the ecosystems of the South American Theraphosdae species. In her presentation, she will outline the effect of the degredation of the Amazon basin has had on the native insect and arachnid species.
Date: 22 March 2016, Doors open at 11am for a 11:30am start.

Return to the top

David Woodrow


author of Theraphosidae in a changing environment

Using examples from his book "Theraphosidae in a Changing Environment", David Woodrow give a fascinating look at the arachnid and their ability to cope with the changes to the global climate. Tackling issues such as destruction of natural habitats, climate change, and the evolutionary differences between the various Theraphosidae subspecies. David will be available after the presentation for a book signing, copies will be available for purchase for $25USD.
Date: 23 March 2016, Doors open at 10:30am for a 11:00am start.

Return to the top

Dr A. Gupta,


Social Psychology lecturer, University of New Delhi
"Improving the Tarantula image"

Dr Gupta combines his extensive knowledge of social psychology and his personal interest in Tarantulas to bring an informative presentation on how to combat the negative image spiders have in the broader community. Through his anecdotes about his arachnophobic father backed by the latest psychological research he to demonstrate how even the most resistant people can learn to love this misrepresented creature.
Date: 24 March 2016, Doors open at 10:00am for a 10:30am start.

Return to the top

Marc Cadotte


Biologist, University of Notre Dame
"Theraphosidae as a potential food source"

Can Tarantulas be friends and food? In a fascinating presentation, Marc Cadotte looks at the macro and micro nutritional breakdown of Theraphosidae. He looks at its potential as a sustitute for more commonly eaten protein sources and explores cultures that already use arachnid and like as a food source.
Date: 25 March 2016, Doors open at 10:00am for a 10:30am start.
